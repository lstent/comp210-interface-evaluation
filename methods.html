Preliminary Questions

Before conducting the main questionnaire I asked 10 individuals to play through the Standby game and comment about the most user unfriendly parts of the game, collecting quantative data such as noting down the length of time the game lasted before the player won or was killed, how many keycards they collected in the time and if they had managed to win the game. Once the game finnished I asked them qualatative questions on what they thought hindered the player most during the game.

UI Questionaire

After comparing the results from the preliminary questions from the 10 player it was obvious the main complaint was the lack of player support throughout the game, with this knowledge I created a questionnaire focusing on player support.

I conducted this questionnaire on 20 individuals within the Falmouth games academy. Before the individuals played the game I made sure to put it on the main menu where the option to view controls was available, and made note if the player looked at the controls screen before attempting to play the game, I then asked the player 6 questions related to player support: 1.How clear are the controls?, 2.Is the aim obvious?, 3.Is the goal UI easily accessable?, 4.Are the interactables difficult to identify?, 5.Can you tell the difference between pick-ups and interactables?, 6.Is it clear what a pick-up item does?, to which they will answer a number from 1 to 5, 5 being no/really bad and 1 is yes/brillient. Questions 1 - 3 are asked while the player is on their first run through of the game, for questions 5 - 6 after the player has finnished there first play, I will direct the player to an area of the game with several interactables and one pick-up and ask them to if they can find and activate an interactable for question 4, for questions 5 and 6, I will tell them there is a pick-up in the same room can they find it, ask them how easily they found it then ask if they know what the pick-up does now that they have it.

After completeing the game depending on there answers I will then conduct an interview asking 10 players how in their oppinion would be the best way to rectify the problem they came accross in game e.g. if they had trouble distinguishing the interactable objects, ask if this was there game how they would change it to make them more obvious or how other games they have played in the past have made interactable objects obvious.
